## The Honest Downsides

No robot vacuum is perfect. Here's where the Q5 Pro falls short:

### Mopping Is Underwhelming

TechRadar's testing was blunt: the Q5 Pro is *"let down by an extremely underwhelming mopping system."*

The mop is a static pad—no vibration, no oscillation, just dragging a wet cloth across the floor. It's fine for light dust maintenance but won't handle sticky spills or dried-on messes.

### Basic Obstacle Avoidance

Unlike premium models with AI cameras, the Q5 Pro relies on bump sensors for obstacles. It'll navigate around furniture fine, but it will run over charging cables, socks, and pet toys.

One reviewer noted: *"While mapping is good, the obstacle avoidance here is basic. If there are often toys or other clutter on your floor, and you don't want to have to tidy before every vacuum session, this is one to avoid."*

### Hair Still Tangles (Sometimes)

Despite the DuoRoller design, owners with long hair report issues: *"What it really has trouble with is my own shoulder-length hair, which just gets wrapped around the brush."*

It's better than bristle brushes, but plan on occasional maintenance.

### WiFi Connectivity Issues

Some users report random disconnects: *"Loved my Roborock Q5 Pro until it disconnected from my WiFi and I wasn't able to get it to connect again."*

This seems to be a minority issue, but worth noting if you have a mesh network or spotty WiFi.
