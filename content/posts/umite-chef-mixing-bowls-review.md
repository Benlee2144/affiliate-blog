## The Honest Downsides

No product is perfect. Here's what you should know before buying.

### Lids Warp in the Dishwasher

This is the most common complaint. While the stainless steel bowls handle dishwasher heat fine, the plastic lids can warp if exposed to high temperatures repeatedly. Multiple owners recommend hand washing the lids to maintain their airtight seal. As one reviewer noted: "The lids might warp slightly if placed in the dishwasher. A gentle hand wash is better for maintaining their shape."

### Not Stand Mixer Compatible

If you have a KitchenAid or similar stand mixer, these bowls won't lock into the base. The silicone bottoms are designed for countertop stability only. One Amazon Q&A response confirmed: "My KitchenAid mixer has a locking device in the base to hold the mixing bowl, so these would not work with that."

### Mirror Finish Shows Fingerprints

The shiny stainless steel interior looks great in photos but can show fingerprints and water spots. The exterior has a satin finish that's more forgiving, but you may find yourself wiping down the insides more often than you'd like.

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Set

1. **Hand wash the lids** — The bowls can go in the dishwasher, but keep lids out to prevent warping.

2. **Use the right grater disc** — Thin slice for garnishes, thick shred for salads, fine grate for zesting and hard cheeses.

3. **Dry bowls completely** — While the stainless steel is rust-resistant, drying the insides after washing prevents water spots and extends the life of the bowls.

4. **Store nested with lids separate** — Stack the bowls together but store lids nearby rather than sealed on. This prevents any seal degradation over time.

5. **Use the 4QT bowl for grating** — It's specifically designed to work with the grater attachments. The removable center lid is a bonus for adding ingredients mid-prep.
