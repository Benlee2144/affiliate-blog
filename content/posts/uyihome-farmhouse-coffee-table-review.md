## Technical Specifications and Materials Science

### MDF Construction Analysis

**Medium-Density Fiberboard advantages:**
- Consistent density eliminates wood grain weak points
- Takes paint and veneer finishes uniformly
- Costs 60-70% less than equivalent solid wood
- Precision cutting enables complex joinery

**MDF limitations for furniture:**
- Moisture sensitivity requires surface protection
- Edge damage cannot be repaired like solid wood
- Heavier than equivalent solid wood construction
- Cannot be refinished multiple times

### Veneer Technology Assessment

**Oil-painted grey oak veneer benefits:**
- Realistic wood grain appearance at fraction of cost
- Anti-scratch coating provides moderate protection
- Consistent color matching across all surfaces
- UV-resistant properties prevent immediate fading

**Veneer durability expectations:**
- 5-10 years normal appearance with care
- Scratches through to substrate are permanent
- Water damage causes irreversible bubbling
- Cannot be sanded and refinished like solid wood

### V-Frame Engineering Analysis

**Triangular stability principles:**
- V-shape creates inherent structural rigidity
- Distributes weight across multiple contact points
- Eliminates single-point failure modes common in 4-leg designs
- Provides visual lightness while maintaining strength

## Maintenance and Care Guide

### Daily Maintenance (30 seconds)

1. **Wipe spills immediately** with dry or slightly damp cloth
2. **Use coasters consistently** to prevent water rings and heat damage
3. **Dust regularly** with microfiber cloth to maintain finish appearance
4. **Rotate decorative items** to prevent uneven sun exposure fading

### Weekly Cleaning (5 minutes)

1. **Vacuum lower shelf slats** to remove accumulated dust and debris
2. **Clean with wood-safe cleaner** designed for veneer finishes
3. **Check hardware tightness** and adjust if any loosening detected
4. **Organize lower shelf storage** to maintain neat appearance

### Seasonal Deep Care

**Every 3 months:**
- Apply furniture polish designed for veneer finishes
- Inspect for wear patterns and address early damage
- Reorganize lower shelf completely for thorough cleaning
- Check for loose joints and re-tighten hardware as needed

**Protection strategies:**
- Use furniture pads under heavy decorative items
- Maintain consistent humidity (30-50%) to prevent veneer issues
- Avoid direct sunlight exposure for extended periods
- Place protective mats under frequently moved items
