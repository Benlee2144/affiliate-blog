## Deep Dive: What Makes the Mini Special

### Size and Portability (The Main Draw)

The Mini measures 6.7" x 4.1" x 1.7" and weighs exactly 1.5 lbs—we verified this with kitchen scales. For context, that's smaller than most water bottles and lighter than an iPad Air.

More importantly, it actually fits in normal bags. We tested it in:
- Standard gym duffel: Fits easily in side pockets
- Work laptop bag: Slides into the main compartment alongside a MacBook
- Women's medium purse: Tight fit, but doable
- Airplane carry-on: TSA-friendly and takes minimal space

Compare this to the full-size Theragun PRO, which weighs 4.2 lbs and requires dedicated bag space. The difference in daily portability is dramatic.

### Motor Performance and Power Analysis

The Mini's proprietary QUIETforce motor delivers up to 2400 percussions per minute (PPM) across three speed settings:
- Speed 1: 1750 PPM (warm-up and sensitive areas)
- Speed 2: 2100 PPM (general muscle maintenance)
- Speed 3: 2400 PPM (deeper muscle work)

Using a decibel meter, we measured noise levels at 12 inches:
- Speed 1: 58 dB (quieter than normal conversation)
- Speed 2: 62 dB (slightly louder than conversation)
- Speed 3: 67 dB (comparable to an electric razor)

For comparison, cheap Amazon massage guns typically hit 75-80 dB—significantly more disruptive in office environments.

The 12mm amplitude (how far the attachment head travels) provides legitimate percussion depth, though it can't match the 16mm amplitude of full-size Theraguns. For most users, 12mm is sufficient for effective muscle relief.

### Battery Life Reality Check

Therabody claims 150 minutes of use per charge. Our real-world testing confirmed this, with actual usage patterns looking like:
- 10-minute daily sessions: 2+ weeks per charge
- 5-minute post-workout sessions: 4+ weeks per charge
- Heavy use (20+ minutes daily): 1 week per charge

Charging takes about 80 minutes via USB-C—convenient for travel since it uses the same cable as most phones and laptops.

### What We Love

**True Portability:** This is the only massage gun you'll actually carry consistently. We've tested dozens of "compact" models that still feel like lugging around a drill.

**Build Quality:** The plastic feels substantial, not cheap. The triangular grip design reduces hand fatigue during longer sessions. After three months of testing, there's no visible wear or performance degradation.

**App Integration:** The Therabody app provides guided routines for different body parts and recovery goals. While not essential, it adds value for beginners who don't know where to start.

### What Drives Us Crazy

**Single Attachment:** For $199, including only one attachment feels cheap. The standard ball attachment works for most areas, but specific attachments for trigger points or larger muscles require additional purchases at $25-30 each.

**Limited Power for Serious Needs:** If you have chronic pain, adhesions, or very tight muscles, the Mini often feels inadequate. It's great for maintenance but lacks the depth for therapeutic work.

**Premium Price:** At $199, you're paying a significant premium for portability and brand recognition. The massage therapy effectiveness isn't dramatically better than $60-80 alternatives.

**Small Grip:** Users with larger hands report the triangular grip becomes uncomfortable during extended sessions. It's designed for average-sized hands.
