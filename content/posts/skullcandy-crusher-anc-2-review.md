Let's be clear: these headphones *vibrate*. Built-in haptic drivers pulse along with bass frequencies, creating a sensation somewhere between wearing headphones and strapping a subwoofer to your head.

![Skullcandy Crusher ANC 2 ear cup design](/images/products/skullcandy-crusher-anc-2-2.jpg)

Multiple reviewers on audio forums describe it as "feeling the kick drum in your chest" or "like standing next to a speaker at a concert." SoundGuys measured approximately **20dB of additional bass output at 50Hz** when the Crusher slider is maxed—that's not subtle.

The intensity is adjustable via a slider on the left ear cup (0-100%). Most users settle around 20-40% for daily listening. At 50%+, multiple reviewers found it "downright unpleasant," comparing it to someone "rhythmically slamming cupped hands over your ears."

**Bottom line:** If you've ever wished you could *feel* your music more, these deliver. If that sounds exhausting, trust your instincts.

## The Honest Downsides

We dug through hundreds of critical reviews. Here's what real owners complain about:

### ANC Is "Just Okay"

Multiple audio sites tested the noise cancellation against Sony and Bose. The verdict? TechRadar called it "pretty weak sauce." TechHive said it "pales in comparison to higher-priced competition."

![Skullcandy Crusher ANC 2 folded for travel](/images/products/skullcandy-crusher-anc-2-3.jpg)

If blocking airplane noise or office chatter is your priority, these aren't the answer. The ANC handles low rumbles decently (ironic, given the bass focus), but higher frequencies like voices bleed through.

### Dynamic Range Compression

Audiophiles on forums report a DSP quirk: "The headphones continuously crush dynamics. Anytime a sound wants to get slightly louder—like a snare hit or bass drop—the DSP briefly lowers the volume."

For casual listeners, this isn't noticeable. For those who appreciate dynamic classical recordings or jazz, it's a dealbreaker.

### The "Hey Skullcandy" Voice Command

Yes, really. The wake word is "Hey Skullcandy," and reviewers universally hate it. AppleInsider called it a "big nope" for public use. Most users disable voice controls entirely.

### Not Water Resistant

Unlike many competitors, the Crusher ANC 2 has no IP rating. Sweaty workouts or light rain could damage them. One frustrated buyer on eBay reported issues after using them at the gym.

### Random Power Issues

A minority of users report the headphones "randomly turning off and back on" during use. Skullcandy's support page acknowledges Bluetooth interference can cause this. A factory reset usually fixes it, but it's worth noting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is the Skullcandy Crusher ANC 2 worth it?**

Yes, if you're a bass lover who wants a unique listening experience. The haptic bass feature is unmatched. However, if you prioritize noise cancellation or balanced audio, the Sony XM5 or Bose QuietComfort are better choices.

**How long does the Crusher ANC 2 battery last?**

Up to 60 hours with ANC off, or 50 hours with ANC on. Fast charging provides 4 hours of playback from just 10 minutes of charging.

**Is the Crusher ANC 2 good for gaming?**

The bass rumble can enhance gaming immersion, but there's noticeable latency in Bluetooth mode. For competitive gaming, wired headphones are better.

**Can you turn off the Crusher bass effect?**

Yes, the bass slider goes from 0% to 100%. At 0%, these function as regular headphones with a warm sound signature.
