## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should I invest in smart home products if I'm not tech-savvy?

Start with simple smart home products like smart plugs and basic smart thermostats. These provide clear benefits without complex setup. Avoid advanced automation systems until you're comfortable with the basics.

### How do I know if an air purifier is actually working?

Look for models with air quality sensors that provide real-time feedback. You should notice reduced dust accumulation and, if you have allergies, symptom improvement. We recommend running purifiers continuously rather than intermittently.

### Are robot vacuums worth it for homes with pets?

Yes, but choose models specifically designed for pet hair. They won't completely replace traditional vacuuming, but they significantly reduce daily pet hair and dander buildup. Empty the bin frequently and clean brushes regularly.

### What's the difference between cheap and expensive dehumidifiers?

Expensive dehumidifiers typically remove moisture more efficiently, run quieter, and last longer. For problem areas like basements, the investment in a quality unit usually pays off through better performance and reliability.

### How often should I replace air purifier filters?

Follow manufacturer recommendations, typically every 6-12 months for HEPA filters. Some models have filter replacement indicators. Don't wait until filters are completely clogged — efficiency drops significantly before filters appear dirty.

### Do smart thermostats really save money?

Quality smart thermostats typically reduce energy costs by 10-15% through better scheduling and optimization. The payback period is usually 1-2 years. The key is choosing a model that learns your schedule automatically rather than requiring manual programming.
