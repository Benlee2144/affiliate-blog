## Why the Oral-B iO Series 7 Stands Out

### Magnetic Oscillating Technology

The iO Series uses a magnetic motor instead of traditional gears. The result: smoother, quieter operation with more consistent power delivery. Your teeth feel professionally cleaned after every use.

Studies slightly favor Oral-B's oscillating-rotating motion over Sonicare's sonic vibrations for plaque removal. The difference isn't dramatic, but it's measurable.

![Oral-B iO Series](/images/products/oral-b-io7-2.jpg)

### AI-Powered Coaching

The companion app uses position detection to show you exactly where you're brushing—and where you're missing. After a week of use, you'll know your blind spots.

The app tracks:
- Coverage of all mouth zones
- Brushing pressure (too hard, too soft, just right)
- Time spent in each quadrant
- Historical trends over weeks and months

### Smart Pressure Sensor

The handle lights up in three colors:
- Green: Perfect pressure
- Yellow: Acceptable
- Red: Too hard—you're damaging your gums

This alone justifies the upgrade. Most people brush too hard without realizing it.

## The Honest Downsides

### Battery Issues Are Real

This is the most common complaint. Users report battery percentage dropping suddenly from 50% to 0%. Reddit's r/oralbtoothbrush is full of similar stories.

One user described it as "clearly a design defect"—their wife's unit had the same issue. If this happens within warranty, Oral-B will replace it.

### Shorter Battery Life Than Advertised

Oral-B claims two weeks. Real-world testing shows 10-11 days with twice-daily brushing. Not terrible, but disappointing for a premium product when some competitors last a month.

### Expensive Brush Heads

iO-specific brush heads cost $12 each vs $5 for regular Oral-B heads. Budget $50-70 per year on replacements (assuming you replace every 3 months as recommended).

### Limited Brush Head Sizes

There's no smaller head option for the iO Series. If you have a small mouth or prefer compact brush heads, this could be frustrating.
