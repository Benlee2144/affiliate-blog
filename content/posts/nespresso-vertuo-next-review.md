## Why Vertuo Works

### Barcode Technology Customizes Everything

Each capsule has a barcode that tells the machine exactly how to brew it—temperature, spin speed, water amount. You can't mess it up.

### Centrifusion Brewing

The Vertuo spins capsules at 7,000 RPM, extracting coffee differently than traditional pressure espresso. The result: rich crema and consistent extraction every time.

### Five Cup Sizes

- Espresso (1.35oz)
- Double Espresso (2.7oz)
- Gran Lungo (5oz)
- Mug (7.7oz)
- Alto (14oz)

One machine covers everything from shots to large mugs.

## The Real Costs

### Per-Cup Math

- Nespresso capsules: $0.85-1.10 each
- Starbucks latte: $5.50
- Home drip coffee: $0.20

At 2 cups daily, Nespresso costs ~$650/year. Cheaper than coffee shops, pricier than drip.

### Is It "Real" Espresso?

Purists say no—centrifugal brewing differs from 9-bar pressure extraction. The taste is close but distinguishable. For lattes and americanos, most won't notice. For straight espresso connoisseurs, it's a compromise.

---

## The Honest Downsides

### Capsule Lock-In

Vertuo capsules only work in Vertuo machines—no third-party alternatives like Original line has. You're locked into Nespresso's ecosystem and pricing.

### Environmental Concerns

Despite recycling programs, capsule waste adds up. Consider this if sustainability is a priority.

### Machine Reliability Reports

Some users report machines failing after 1-2 years. Nespresso's customer service is generally responsive, but it's worth noting.
